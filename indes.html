Mathematics

Sets

Definition

Sets are well-defined collections of distinct objects called elements.

Notes

Union, Intersection, Difference, Complement.

Exercises

  • Find the union and intersection of A={1,2,3} and B={2,3,4}.
  • Find the complement of C={1,2,3} in U={1,2,3,4,5}.

Arithmetic Progression

Definition

An AP is a sequence where each term after the first is obtained by adding a common difference.

Notes

Formula: a_n = a_1 + (n-1)d

Exercises

  • Find the 10th term of 2,5,8,11,...
  • Sum of first 20 terms of 3,7,11,...

Quadratic Equations

Definition

Equations of the form ax² + bx + c = 0.

Notes

Solving using factorization, completing the square, or quadratic formula.

Exercises

  • Solve x² - 5x + 6 = 0.
  • Use quadratic formula to solve 2x² - 3x - 5 = 0.

Sequence & Series

Definition

Sequences are ordered lists of numbers; series is their sum.

Notes

Arithmetic & geometric sequences and series formulas.

Exercises

  • Find the sum of first 10 terms of 2,4,6,...
  • Determine 5th term of geometric sequence 3,6,12,...

Statistics

Definition

Collection, analysis, interpretation, and presentation of data.

Notes

Mean, Median, Mode, Range, Standard Deviation.

Exercises

  • Find mean, median, mode of 2,4,6,8,10.
  • Find the range of 5,9,12,7,3.

Physics

Fundamental & Derived Units

Definition

Fundamental units are basic measurements; derived units are combinations.

Notes

Length = m, Mass = kg, Time = s; Speed = m/s, Force = kg·m/s²

Exercises

  • List three fundamental units.
  • Express force in fundamental units.

Laws of Motion

Definition

Newton's laws of motion describe the relationship between motion and forces.

Notes

1st Law: Inertia; 2nd Law: F=ma; 3rd Law: Action-Reaction.

Exercises

  • Give an example of Newton's 1st law.
  • Calculate force for m=5kg, a=2m/s².

Work, Power & Energy

Definition

Work done = Force × Distance; Power = Work/Time; Energy = capacity to do work.

Notes

Kinetic & potential energy, mechanical work.

Exercises

  • Calculate work done by a 10N force over 5m.
  • Find power if 50J work is done in 10s.

Light & Optics

Definition

Study of light propagation, reflection, refraction, and lenses.

Notes

Laws of reflection, Snell's law, focal length of lenses.

Exercises

  • Calculate image distance for a lens with f=10cm and u=15cm.
  • Explain total internal reflection.

Electricity & Magnetism

Definition

Study of electric charges, currents, fields, and magnets.

Notes

Ohm’s law, Coulomb's law, magnetic fields, circuits.

Exercises

  • Find current if V=12V and R=4Ω.
  • Calculate force between two charges of 2C and 3C separated by 1m.

Chemistry

Introduction to Chemistry

Definition

Chemistry is the study of matter, its properties, and changes.

Notes

Branches: Organic, Inorganic, Physical, Analytical.

Exercises

  • List the main branches of chemistry.
  • Give 2 examples of chemical changes.

States of Matter

Definition

Matter exists in solid, liquid, and gas states.

Notes

Changes: Melting, Freezing, Vaporization, Condensation, Sublimation.

Exercises

  • Give an example of sublimation.
  • Explain the change of state from liquid to gas.

Atomic Structure

Definition

Atoms consist of protons, neutrons, and electrons.

Notes

Electron configuration, isotopes, mass number.

Exercises

  • Determine the number of protons and electrons in O atom.
  • Define isotopes of hydrogen.

Periodic Table

Definition

Arrangement of elements by atomic number and properties.

Notes

Groups, periods, metals, non-metals, noble gases.

Exercises

  • Identify the group of oxygen.
  • Find period number of Na.

Chemical Bonding

Definition

Atoms combine by sharing or transferring electrons to form compounds.

Notes

Ionic, covalent, and metallic bonds.

Exercises

  • Give examples of ionic and covalent compounds.
  • Explain metallic bonding in sodium.
